# China visa medical check vs private full body checkup: what foreigners should know

Foreigners often search for a full body checkup in China and assume it can also support a work visa, student visa, or residence permit. In many cases, the official health certificate path is different from a private checkup package sold by hospitals or clinics.

## They are not the same service

An official visa or residence health check is usually connected to a specific administrative requirement. It may need to be completed at a designated center, follow a local checklist, and produce a certificate or report format accepted by the relevant school, employer, or authority.

A private full body checkup is different. It is usually a self-pay screening package chosen for personal health awareness, preventive screening, or annual review. It may include useful tests, but it does not automatically replace an official China visa or residence medical check.

## Why overseas reports can still lead to a re-check

An overseas exam may be missing required items, signatures, stamps, original reports, or verification rules used by the local China International Travel Healthcare Center. Before paying abroad, ask your school or employer whether a China-issued or China-verified certificate is required.

Even when an overseas report looks complete, local execution can differ by city, purpose, and receiving institution. A report that was accepted in one case may not be accepted in another.

## Best next step

Start by identifying your purpose: residence permit, work onboarding, school registration, annual screening, or travel health check. Then choose the booking path, documents, report language, and support level around that purpose.

If you are unsure, ask these questions before booking:

- Is this for a visa, residence permit, employer, school, or personal screening?
- Does the result need to come from a designated China health check center?
- Will an overseas report be accepted, verified, or rejected?
- Do you need English support for booking, registration, report pickup, or follow-up questions?
